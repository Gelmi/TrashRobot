Key takeaways

  • Sample: 20 participants, within-subject comparison of justification vs no justification.
  • Compliance when the robot is correct: pooled rate was 68.1% with justification vs 60.6% without justification.
  • Override when the robot is incorrect: pooled rate was 4.4% with justification vs 12.5% without justification.
  • Memory accuracy: nearly identical across conditions (87.5% vs 88.1%).
  • Timing: average arm time was much lower with justification (4.61s vs 12.25s), while average speaker time was higher (3.97s vs 3.05s).
  • Trust decay: depending on aggregation, the pre/post pattern is mixed, so it is worth checking both participant-level means and pooled trial totals.
